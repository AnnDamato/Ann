1922


Ann D'Amato Artell was born in New Milford Connecticut and the rest is history

In 1939


she graduated from Norwalk High School.

In 1960


she started to mentor some local teenagers who have since grown to national prominence. These child prodigies include Anthony Dolan a Pulitzer prize winning former journalist who later became a speech writer for Ronald Reagan; and late Terry Dolan former leader of National Conservative Political Action Committee that helped shepherd Reagan into the presidency in 1980.

In 1964


Ann served as vice chairman of the Norwalk GOP committee. She was a VIP guide in the Vatican Pavilion at the World’s Fair of 1964-65 as a representative of the Bridgeport Diocese.

In 1965


she founded her own real estate business Quality Homes which she operated for fifth teen years..

In 1968


she attended the Republican National Convention in Miami reporting back to the The Hour.

In 1970


she assumed leadership of the District A committee where she worked for 23 years in leadership positions.

In 1973


she received a certificate of merit from The Columbiettes for her untiring efforts and devoted service.

In 1974


she became the president of the Norwalk Business and professional Women’s Club.

In 1977


She became the registrar of voter in Norwalk which she held for 24 years.

In 1980


she served as a delegate to the National Republican Convention which nominated Reagan for president. During this time she was a member of the National Committee for Ronald Reagan and Chairman of Connecitcut Coordinator for the primary and the election in 1980

In 1981


she was honored at the 11th Annual Red, White, and Blue Ball where she received the Americanism Award.

In 1982


she was honored as the Norwalk Business and Professional Woman of the Year.

In 1983


she received a Certificate of Award from the Norwalk Republican Women’s Association for outstanding service to the Republican Party.

In 2002


she received a Certificate Of Appreciation from The Columbiettes for 40 years of membership and service.

In 2003


she was a member at large on the Girl Scout Council of Southwestern Connecticut.
